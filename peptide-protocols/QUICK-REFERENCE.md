## Reconstitution Quick Guide

### Standard Reconstitution (2ml BAC Water)

| Vial Size | Water Volume | Concentration |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| 2mg | 2ml | 1mg/ml = 1000mcg/ml |
| 5mg | 2ml | 2.5mg/ml = 2500mcg/ml |
| 10mg | 2ml | 5mg/ml = 5000mcg/ml |
| 100mg | 2ml | 50mg/ml |
| 500mg | 2ml | 250mg/ml |

### Quick Dose Calculations

**For 5mg in 2ml (2500mcg/ml):**
- 100mcg = 4 units
- 250mcg = 10 units
- 500mcg = 20 units
- 1mg = 40 units

**For 10mg in 2ml (5000mcg/ml):**
- 100mcg = 2 units
- 250mcg = 5 units
- 500mcg = 10 units
- 1mg = 20 units
- 2mg = 40 units

## Injection Site Reference

### Subcutaneous (SC) Sites
✓ Abdomen (most common)
✓ Front/outer thigh
✓ Back of upper arm
✓ Upper buttocks

**Technique:** Pinch skin, 45-90° angle

### Intramuscular (IM) Sites
✓ Deltoid (shoulder) - small volumes
✓ Vastus lateralis (thigh)
✓ Gluteus (buttock)
✓ Ventrogluteal (hip)

**Technique:** No pinch, 90° angle

## Storage Quick Reference

### Before Reconstitution
- **Temperature:** -20°C (freezer)
- **Shelf Life:** 1-2 years
- **Protection:** Keep dark, dry, sealed

### After Reconstitution
- **Temperature:** 2-8°C (refrigerator)
- **Shelf Life:** 2-4 weeks (most peptides)
- **Protection:** Keep dark, upright
- **Never:** Freeze reconstituted peptides

## Troubleshooting Quick Fixes

### Problem: Injection Hurts
**Solutions:**
- Use more dilute solution
- Inject slower
- Ice area before
- Try different site
- Room temperature peptide

### Problem: Not Dissolving
**Solutions:**
- Wait longer (30 min)
- Gentle swirling
- Slight warmth from hands
- Never shake

### Problem: Nausea (GLP-1s)
**Solutions:**
- Eat smaller meals
- Slow titration
- Take with food
- Stay hydrated
- Ginger tea

### Problem: Can't Remember Schedule
**Solutions:**
- Phone alarms
- Pill organizer for supplies
- Calendar reminders
- Habit stacking
- Written log

## When to Seek Professional Guidance

### Consult Healthcare Provider Before Starting If:
- You have serious medical conditions
- You take multiple medications
- You're pregnant or nursing
- You have history of cancer
- You have endocrine disorders
- You're under 25 years old

### Consult During Use If:
- Severe or persistent side effects
- Unexpected reactions
- Questions about interactions
- Need for blood work interpretation
- Concerns about effectiveness

### Emergency Medical Attention If:
- Severe allergic reaction
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Severe swelling
- Signs of infection with fever
