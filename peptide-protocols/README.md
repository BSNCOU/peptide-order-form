3. **Reconstitution**: Use bacteriostatic water (BAC water) unless otherwise specified

## General Guidelines

### Reconstitution Process

1. Remove peptide vial from refrigeration and allow to reach room temperature
2. Clean the rubber stopper with an alcohol swab
3. Draw the appropriate amount of bacteriostatic water into a sterile syringe
4. Inject the water slowly down the side of the vial (not directly onto the powder)
5. Gently swirl (do not shake) until powder is fully dissolved
6. Label the vial with reconstitution date
7. Store reconstituted peptide in refrigerator (2-8¬∞C)

### Administration Guidelines

- Use insulin syringes (typically 0.5ml or 1ml)
- Subcutaneous injection is most common route
- Rotate injection sites to prevent tissue damage
- Follow proper injection technique
- Maintain injection log for tracking

## Storage Guidelines

### Lyophilized (Powder) Storage
- Temperature: -20¬∞C to -80¬∞C
- Shelf life: 1-2 years when properly stored
- Keep away from light and moisture

### Reconstituted Storage
- Temperature: 2-8¬∞C (refrigerator)
- Shelf life: 2-4 weeks (varies by peptide)
- Protect from light
- Do not freeze

## Dosage Calculation

### Example Calculation

If you have a 5mg vial and reconstitute with 2ml of BAC water:
- 5mg = 5000mcg
- 5000mcg / 2ml = 2500mcg/ml
- 2500mcg/ml / 100 units = 25mcg per unit on insulin syringe
